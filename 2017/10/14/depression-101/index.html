What do we know about depression?

关于抑郁症，我们都知道什么？

  1. 抑郁情绪不等于抑郁症。前者每个人都有可能遇到，后者是严重的精神疾病。
  2. The history and science of depression, by Siddhartha Mukherjeeapril in New York Time at April 19, 2012. Post-Prozac Nation
    1. Another best-seller from the same author The Emperor of All Maladies: A Biography of Cancer 众病之王 。
  3. The neurobiology of depression:
    1. one lecture, which is given by Stanford Professor Robert Sapolsky.
    2. one paper, which is entitled as Disner SG, Beevers CG, Haigh EAP, Beck AT (2011). Neural mechanisms of the cognitive model of depression. Nature Reviews Neuroscience 12: 467–477.
      1. Biased Attention
      2. Biased Processing
      3. Biased Memory
  4. A fundamental model of depression by Dr. Helen Mayberg.

    Mayberg HS (1997). Limbic-cortical dysregulation: a proposed model of depression. J Neuropsychiatry Clin Neurosci 9: 471–481.

  5. The hyperconnectivity of depression (in default mode network (DMN)) during resting state.

    1. The first paper proposed this hypothesis.

      Greicius MD, Flores BH, Menon V, Glover GH, Solvason HB, Kenna H, et al (2007). Resting-State Functional Connectivity in Major Depression: Abnormally Increased Contributions from Subgenual Cingulate Cortex and Thalamus. Biological Psychiatry 62: 429–437.

    2. Confirmed by this paper, and further highlights the importance of dorsomedial prefrontal cortex or known as dorsal nexus.

      Sheline YI, Price JL, Yan Z, Mintun MA (2010). Resting-state functional MRI in depression unmasks increased connectivity between networks via the dorsal nexus. Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences 107: 11020–11025.

    3. A meta-analysis on the cross-networks dysfunctions.

      Kaiser RH, Andrews-Hanna JR, Wager TD, Pizzagalli DA (2015). Large-Scale Network Dysfunction in Major Depressive Disorder: A Meta-analysis of Resting-State Functional Connectivity. JAMA Psychiatry 72: 603.

  6. A most promising target for depression treatment - the rostral anterior cingulate cortex (rACC), also know as ‘pregenual’ or ‘perigenual’ ACC.

    1. A meta-analysis for the special role of pgACC in neuropathology of depression.

      Pizzagalli DA (2011). Frontocingulate dysfunction in depression: toward biomarkers of treatment response. Neuropsychopharmacology 36: 183–206.
